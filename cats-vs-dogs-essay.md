# Why Cats Are Better Than Dogs

For millennia, humanity has debated the merits of its two most beloved domestic companions: the cat and the dog. While dogs have earned their reputation as "man's best friend," a closer examination reveals that cats are, in fact, the superior pet.

First, cats respect boundaries. A dog greets you at the door with frantic enthusiasm, demanding attention with wet licks and an insistent tail. A cat, by contrast, acknowledges your presence with quiet dignity — perhaps a slow blink from across the room. This restraint is not aloofness; it is respect. Cats understand that love need not be suffocating. They give you space to breathe, and in return, their affection feels earned rather than demanded.

Second, cats are self-sufficient. A dog requires walks, constant supervision, and near-endless entertainment. A cat requires food, water, a clean litter box, and a sunbeam. For the modern person juggling work, travel, and social commitments, the cat's independence is a practical blessing. Leave a dog alone for twelve hours and you return to chaos. Leave a cat alone and you return to a slightly displeased sovereign who will forgive you once you open a can of tuna.

Third, cats are cleaner. They groom themselves meticulously, bury their waste, and do not track mud through the house. They do not bark at passersby, drool on your furniture, or require baths. A cat's purr is a therapeutic vibration proven to reduce stress, and their quiet presence brings calm rather than commotion.

Finally, there is grace. A cat moves through the world like poetry — every leap, stretch, and landing a study in elegance. Dogs, for all their charm, are clumsy, awkward creatures in comparison.

The cat asks for little and gives much: companionship without clinginess, beauty without vanity, and love on its own terms. And that, precisely, is why it is better.
